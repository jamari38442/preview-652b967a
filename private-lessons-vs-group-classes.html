Private Lessons vs. Group Classes: Which Is Right for Your Dog?

Canine Intuition · Private Lessons & Group Classes

Both formats work. The question is which one matches where your dog is right now, and what you're actually trying to fix.

Choose private lessons if...

  • Your dog pulls hard on the leash, jumps on guests, or ignores basic commands at home.
  • You're dealing with mild to moderate behavioral issues that need one-on-one attention.
  • You want a program built entirely around your schedule and your dog's specific triggers.
  • Your dog gets overstimulated or reactive around other dogs, making a group setting too much too soon.

Choose group classes if...

  • Your dog already has basic obedience but struggles with social skills around other dogs.
  • Your dog gets overwhelmed or "goes crazy" at the dog park and needs structured practice around distractions.
  • You'd like to meet other owners working through similar challenges.
  • You're raising a puppy and want to build positive experiences with other dogs early.

Not sure which one fits?

That's exactly what a free consult is for. Tell us what's going on with your dog day to day, and we'll recommend the format that gets you results fastest, sometimes that's a mix of both.
