How Much Does Dog Training Cost in South Florida?

Canine Intuition · Getting Started

Dog training pricing varies a lot, and it's rarely apples to apples. Here's what actually drives the cost, so you can compare quotes with more context.

Format matters

Private lessons are priced per session or per package since they're one-on-one. Group classes are typically priced per dog for a set number of sessions, since the cost is shared across the class. Board & Train is priced as a full program since your dog is in-house for one to three weeks or more.

Length and intensity of the program

A two-week on-leash foundation program costs less than a three-week on/off-leash program, which costs less than an Intensive Behavior Modification program built around aggression or severe anxiety. More time and more specialized handling means more cost.

The trainer's experience level

Rehabilitation work, especially aggression cases, benefits from real experience. A trainer who has evaluated and worked with over a thousand dogs is going to price differently than someone just starting out, and for behavior cases, that experience is what keeps you and your dog safe.

What's included

Ask whether the price includes follow-up sessions, a handoff lesson, or ongoing support after the program ends. A lower price with no follow-up can end up costing more if the training doesn't hold once your dog is home.

Get an exact number for your dog

Because programs are customized to your dog's needs, we don't publish blanket pricing. The fastest way to get an accurate quote is a free consult, where we'll ask about your dog's age, behavior, and goals and recommend (and price) the right program.
