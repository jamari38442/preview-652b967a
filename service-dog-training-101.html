Service Dog Training 101: What Tasks Can Be Trained?

Canine Intuition · Service Dog Training

Service dog training is task-specific work built on top of a very solid obedience foundation. Here's what that actually covers.

Diabetic alert

Dogs are trained to recognize scent changes associated with blood sugar swings and alert their handler before a medical event.

Seizure response

This can include alerting to a seizure in progress, staying with the handler during one, or going to get help.

Mobility support

Tasks like bracing, counterbalance, or helping with balance for handlers who need physical support while moving.

Retrievals

Picking up and delivering dropped items, medication, or a phone, useful for handlers with limited mobility.

PTSD alerts and support

Interrupting anxiety behaviors, creating space in crowded areas, and providing grounding pressure during an episode.

Where training starts

Before any task work begins, we evaluate the dog's temperament and existing obedience level. Task training only sticks on top of a dog that already has reliable focus, impulse control, and public manners. If your dog isn't there yet, we'll build that foundation first, often through our Board & Train or Private Lessons programs.
