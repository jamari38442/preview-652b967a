Why this window matters

Puppies are naturally more open to new experiences before 4 to 6 months old. What feels novel and interesting now can feel threatening later if they never encounter it early. That's the entire idea behind socialization: controlled, positive exposure while they're still wired to accept it.

Keep it positive, not overwhelming

The goal isn't to expose your puppy to everything at once. Short, calm, positive sessions beat a chaotic dog park visit every time. If your puppy seems nervous, back off, give them space, and try again at a smaller scale.

Our Group Classes are built around exactly this: safe, structured socialization with other puppies and owners in the same boat.
